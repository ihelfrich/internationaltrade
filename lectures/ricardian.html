Ricardian Model — Full Lecture

Comparative advantage, specialization, and gains from trade.

Motivation Setup Autarky Trade Gains Worked Example

1) Motivation (the big idea)

Here’s the core punchline: trade patterns are driven by opportunity costs, not absolute costs. That means a country can be worse at producing everything and still gain from trade. This is the logic you want students to internalize before we ever draw a PPF.

Comparative advantage is a statement about relative efficiency, not absolute productivity.

2) Setup (intuition + math)

Intuition

  • Two goods: cloth (C) and wine (W).
  • One factor: labor.
  • Perfect competition, constant returns.
  • Labor is mobile within a country, immobile across countries.

Math

\(Q_C = L_C/a_{LC}, \; Q_W = L_W/a_{LW}\)
\(L_C + L_W = L\)
\(a_{LC}Q_C + a_{LW}Q_W \le L\)

3) Autarky equilibrium

The PPF is linear. Solve for it explicitly:

\(Q_C = \frac{L}{a_{LC}} - \frac{a_{LW}}{a_{LC}} Q_W\)

The slope is \(-a_{LW}/a_{LC}\). That is the opportunity cost of wine in terms of cloth.

Competitive wages

\(w = P_C/a_{LC} = P_W/a_{LW}\)

With labor mobile across sectors, wages must equalize. Hence autarky prices satisfy:

\(P_W/P_C = a_{LW}/a_{LC}\)

4) Trade equilibrium

Compare opportunity costs across countries. Home has comparative advantage in cloth if:

\(\frac{a_{LC}}{a_{LW}} < \frac{a^*_{LC}}{a^*_{LW}}\)

Trade prices must lie between autarky prices:

\(\frac{a_{LW}}{a_{LC}} < (P_W/P_C)^{trade} < \frac{a^*_{LW}}{a^*_{LC}}\)

Given linear PPFs, we get complete specialization: Home exports cloth, Foreign exports wine.

5) Gains from trade

  • Production specializes at a PPF endpoint.
  • Consumption occurs on a world-price line flatter than the PPF.
  • Consumption possibilities expand beyond autarky.
In the Ricardian model, all workers gain because labor is the only factor.
\(w/P_C = 1/a_{LC}\), \(w/P_W = (P_C/P_W)(1/a_{LC})\)

Trade raises real wages in terms of the imported good.

6) Worked Example

Home: \(a_{LC}=1, a_{LW}=2\) → OC of W = 2C

Foreign: \(a^*_{LC}=2, a^*_{LW}=3\) → OC of W = 1.5C

Home has CA in cloth. Trade price satisfies \(1.5 < P_W/P_C < 2\).
  1. Compute opportunity costs for each country.
  2. Compare ratios to identify CA.
  3. Bound the world price between autarky prices.
  4. Infer specialization and gains.
